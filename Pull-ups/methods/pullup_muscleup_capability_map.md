# Pull-up → Muscle-up Capability Map

Progression System (Project Tool)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Overview

This document visualizes the capability progression required to move
from basic pull-ups to a strict muscle-up.

It helps identify:

-   Which stage you are currently in
-   What abilities are missing
-   What training focus is required next

# Stage 1 --- Pull-up Foundation (5 → 8)

Primary limitations:

-   Strength endurance
-   Top-end control

Key exercises:

Pull-up volume training\
Slow negative pull-ups\
Top holds

Typical weekly volume:

50--60 pull-ups

Goal:

Strict Pull-up ≥ 8

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Stage 2 --- High Pull Strength (8 → 10)

Most athletes stall here.

The problem is usually **pulling height**, not basic strength.

Required capability:

Chest-to-Bar pull-ups

Training focus:

Pull-up 5×5\
Chest-to-Bar 4×3\
Explosive Pull-up 4×2

Goal:

Strict Pull-up ≥ 10\
Chest-to-Bar ≥ 5

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Stage 3 --- High Pull

Muscle-ups require pulling much higher than a standard pull-up.

Target height:

Bar → lower chest

Training:

High Pull 5×2

Characteristics:

Explosive\
Tight core\
Fast pull

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Stage 4 --- Transition

The muscle-up is primarily a **transition technique**.

Key drills:

Low bar muscle-up transition\
Band muscle-up

Structure:

5×3

Key cues:

Lean forward\
Wrist turnover\
Elbows over bar

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Stage 5 --- Muscle-up

Minimum capability threshold:

Pull-up ≥ 10\
Dip ≥ 15\
Chest-to-Bar ≥ 5

When these are achieved, the first muscle-up usually appears quickly
with transition practice.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Diagnostic Check

Once you reach **8 pull-ups**, test the following:

Can your chest approach the bar?

If not:

Your limitation is **pull height**, not pull-up count.

Training should shift toward:

Chest-to-Bar\
High Pull

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Key Principle

Pull-ups alone do not produce a muscle-up.

You must develop:

Pull strength\
Explosive pulling\
Transition strength\
Dip strength
